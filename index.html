About Me

Hi, I'm Dennie, an indie game developer currently studying Computing Science at Rutgers University–New Brunswick. I’ve been passionate about game development since I was 15, and ever since, I’ve been exploring every part of the process. Whether it’s pixel art, 3D modeling, or gameplay programming, I’m always experimenting and learning new things to grow as a developer. I enjoy taking on creative and technical challenges, and I’m driven by a love for crafting fun, meaningful experiences.

I’ve worked on a wide range of projects that blend different styles, genres, and mechanics. From reimagining classic games with HD-2D visuals to designing tactical RPG systems that bridge open-world exploration with grid-based combat, I’m always looking for ways to bring fresh ideas to life. My toolkit includes Unreal Engine 5, Unity, Blender, and Aseprite, which I use to bring together both the artistic and programming sides of my projects. Feel free to explore my portfolio and see how I bring creative concepts into playable form.
